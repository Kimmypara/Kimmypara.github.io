CHRISTMAS CAKE

Christmas Cake is a traditional fruit cake with a rich, velvety texture that’s so full flavoured and moist it can be eaten plain. Usually Christmas Cakes need to be started the day before, with overnight soaking of dried fruit. But not mine!

INGREDIENTS

500g Self Raising Flour
6 Eggs
375g margerine
2 bottles Lacto
300g brown sugar
4tbsp treacle
Juice of 1 lemon, 1 orange and 1 tangerine
Lemon and orange zest
1/2 tsp cinnammon powder
2 tbsp cocoa powder
200g dates
100g glaced cherries
500g black currants
300g sultana
200g hazelnuts

METHOD

  1. Place all the dried fruit,cherries, juices, zest, and Lacto in a large bowl and let soak for 1hour.
  2. Preheat oven to 150°C. Grease and line a 21 – 22 cm / 8 – 9" round cake pan with baking paper (parchment paper) (7 cm / 2.75" tall).
  3. In an electric mixer, mix the butter and brown sugar with a K Beater until creamy and fluffy.
  4. Slowly, add the treacle and the eggs 1 at a time.
  5. Sift the flour and cocoa and add slowly to mixture until combined. Do not overbeat.
  6. With a wooden spoon, add the dried fruit mixture and mix until combined.
  7. Bake in oven for around 2 1/2 hours or until a skewer inserted into middle comes out clean with no batter on it.
  8. Remove from oven and cool for 20 minutes before transferring to cooling rack. Cool completely before serving.

Spice-Glazed Turkey

Try a twist on your turkey this Christmas with a fabulous spicy glaze. You can serve with candied carrots and festive sides of your choice.

INGREDIENTS

5.5kg fresh turkey
50g softened butter
Small bunch of thyme
Around 10 leaves of bay leaves
8 carrots, peeled and cut into large chunks
2 star anise, crushed
½ tsp ground cloves
1 tsp ground nutmeg
1 tsp ground ginger
1 tsp ground black pepper
2 tsp light muscovado sugar

FOR BASTE

50g light muscovado sugar
50g maple syrup
50g melted butter
100ml cider vinegar

METHOD

  1. Mix all the spices together and divide in 2 portions and put in seperate bowls.
  2. In the first bowl, mix 2 tsp light muscovado sugar with the spices.
  3. Put the turkey in a roasting dish and season with the spices under the turkey skin.
  4. Let it set in the refrigirator for at least 1 hour.
  5. Put the remaining spice mix and the baste ingredients in a saucepan and heat until the sugar has melted, then set aside.
  6. Smear the turkey all over with the butter and put the herbs on top.
  7. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Loosely cover with foil and roast for at least 3½ hrs.
  8. After 30 minutes of roasting, pour over the spiced baste, then baste again every 30 minutes.
  9. In the final hour, remove the foil and put the carrots in the dish, stir to coat in the juices and continue to cook.

Ginger Chocolate Cupcakes

These Ginger Chocolate Cupcakes are delicious, Christmassy and amazingly light. They are ideal for Christmas meal dessert.

INGREDIENTS

240ml vegetable oil
1 cup golden syrup
1½ cup packed brown sugar
4 large eggs
½ cup buttermilk
2 cups self-raising flour
1 cup cocoa powder
1½ tsp baking soda
1tsp salt
6tsp fresh grated ginger
2tsp cinnamon
½ tsp ground cloves
½ tsp nutmeg

FOR CREAM FROSTING

250ml double cream light(must be cold)
4tbsp icing sugar
1tsp vanilla
4tbsp chocolate chips
1tsp cinnamon powder

METHOD

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 180°C
  2. In a saucepan over low heat, melt the oil, golden syrup, brown sugar with ¼ cup water until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Let cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour the mixture in an electric mixer(be sure it is not hot to avoid scrambling the eggs) and add the buttermilk and eggs. Whisk until all ingredients are combined.
  5. Add the dry ingredients slowly to wet mixture. DO NOT OVERBEAT.
  6. Place cupcake liners in a cupcake mould tray and pour the batter evenly.
  7. Bake for around 20 minutes or until skewer is clean.
  8. For the cream frosting, pour the cold double cream in a bowl with the vanilla extract.
  9. Whisk for around 1 minute and add the icing sugar slowly.
  10. Keep beating until the cream is thick enough for piping DO NOT OVERBEAT.
  11. Once the cupcakes are cold, put the frosting in a piping bag and decorate.
  12. Sprinkle some cinnamon powder and chocolate chips.
